# Code of Conduct

Tilia contributors are expected to keep project spaces welcoming, respectful,
and focused on constructive technical collaboration.

## Expected behavior

- Treat other participants with patience and respect.
- Give actionable technical feedback and assume good faith.
- Welcome people regardless of background, identity, experience, or ability.
- Respect privacy and avoid publishing another person's private information.
- Accept responsibility, apologize, and repair harm when mistakes occur.

Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized attention, personal attacks,
deliberate intimidation, and sustained disruption are not acceptable in any
project space or when representing the project publicly.

## Enforcement

For ordinary technical disagreements, use the relevant issue or pull request.
Report sensitive conduct concerns privately through a
[GitHub security advisory](https://github.com/thimotedupuch/Tilia.jl/security/advisories/new).
Maintainers will review reports confidentially, avoid conflicts of interest,
and may remove content, issue warnings, restrict participation, or permanently
exclude a participant when appropriate. Retaliation against reporters is not
permitted.

This policy applies to the repository, issue tracker, review discussions,
documentation, community events, and other spaces officially associated with
Tilia.
